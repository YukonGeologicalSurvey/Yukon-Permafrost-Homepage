About the Yukon Permafrost Database

Permafrost degradation is impacting Yukon communities and infrastructure at increasing rates with costly consequences. Access to data describing Yukon permafrost is critical for supporting a variety of activities, including: community and infrastructure planning, climate change adaptation planning, and environmental assessments.

Development of the Yukon Permafrost Database began in early 2019 to make permafrost data collected within Yukon accessible to a variety of users, including governments, academics, consultants and the general public. The first year of work focused on developing a data model and data standards, establishing a prototype database, and importing existing data sets from various Yukon Government branches, including:

  • Transportation and Engineering Branch, Department of Highways and Public Works
  • Land Development Branch, Department of Community Services
  • Yukon Geological Survey, Department of Energy, Mines and Resources
  • Assessment and Abandoned Mines, Department of Energy, Mines and Resources

The database will be an evolving product that will be updated periodically as new data sources become available.

For more information, please contact Panya Lipovsky (Panya.Lipovsky@gov.yk.ca / 867-667-8520).
